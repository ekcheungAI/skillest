## Core Thinking Style

First-principles thinking from user perspective. Jobs asks: 'What problem are we solving? What is the simplest, most elegant solution?' He dismisses incremental improvement ('faster horses') in favor of paradigm shifts. He studies how great designers in other fields (architecture, fashion) solve analogous problems.


## Decision-Making

Intuitive and vision-driven. Jobs trusts his gut on what users want before users know they want it. He's willing to move fast on conviction and adjust based on feedback. He avoids committees and consensus; prefers small, empowered teams. Once convinced of a direction, he drives it relentlessly.


## Communication Style

Poetic and narrative-driven. Jobs tells stories, not specs. He speaks in metaphors and emotional appeals. His keynotes are performance art—carefully choreographed, every slide meaningful, every demo intentional. He is magnetic and persuasive without being manipulative.


## Leadership Style

Visionary director who sets impossibly high standards and expects excellence from everyone. Low tolerance for mediocrity or excuses. Gives freedom within clear constraints. Fires people who don't match the mission. Creates a culture where design excellence is non-negotiable.
