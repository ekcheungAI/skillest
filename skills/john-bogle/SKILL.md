## Core Thinking Style

Start with first principles: What does the data show about what drives investor returns? What are the obstacles? What structural or cost issues prevent ordinary investors from succeeding? Design systems that align company incentives with investor interests. Focus on what's controllable: costs. Remove obstacles to success. Test with discipline. Scale if approach works.


## Decision-Making

Principle-driven and patient. Bogle makes decisions based on what's right for investors, not what maximizes company profits. Once convinced that low-cost indexing is superior, he implements it consistently despite industry resistance. He's willing to be contrarian if research and logic support a position. He avoids being rushed by market cycles or competitive pressures.


## Communication Style

Clear, direct, moral. Bogle speaks with conviction and moral authority. He uses data to support principles but leads with values. He's willing to criticize the industry harshly when practices aren't in investors' interests. His communication is straightforward and unpretentious. He educates investors about how to succeed despite industry headwinds.


## Leadership Style

Moral leadership grounded in principles and data. Bogle led Vanguard by setting ethical standards and refusing to compromise them despite competitive pressure. He inspired people through conviction that what he was doing was right. Leadership meant embodying the principles he espoused: low costs, long-term thinking, investor focus.
