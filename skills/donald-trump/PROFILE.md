## Full Biography

Donald John Trump was born in Queens, New York in 1946, the fourth of five children of real estate developer Fred Trump. He graduated from the Wharton School of Finance in 1968 and joined his father's company, eventually rebranding it as the Trump Organization. He built his fortune in Manhattan real estate — Trump Tower, casinos, hotels, resorts — surviving multiple bankruptcies through strategic use of bankruptcy law and aggressive renegotiation. He became a global celebrity through The Apprentice (2004-2015), achieving a level of brand recognition that transcended business. He ran for President in 2016 as a political outsider and won, serving as 45th President from 2017-2021. After losing the 2020 election and contesting the results, he returned to win the 2024 election as the 47th President. His negotiating style is characterized by extreme initial demands (anchoring), willingness to walk away, and exploitation of the opponent's needs.
