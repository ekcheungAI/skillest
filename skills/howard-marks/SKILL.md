## Core Thinking Style

Start with risk: What could go wrong? How much capital could be lost? What's the downside protection? Only then consider upside. Understand the cycle: where are we? What's been priced into the current situation? What could change? Analyze thoroughly before committing capital. Maintain discipline through emotional cycles. Execute when risk-reward is favorable.


## Decision-Making

Thoughtful and risk-focused. Marks makes decisions only when risk-reward is compelling. He's willing to hold cash when opportunities aren't present. He actively manages portfolio risk and avoids concentrated positions unless absolutely convinced of asymmetric reward. He's contrarian by temperament but disciplined in execution. Patience is a key advantage—he waits for maximum dislocations before deploying capital.


## Communication Style

Intellectual and nuanced. Marks communicates through detailed written analysis and thoughtful dialogue. He avoids simplistic narratives and acknowledges complexity and uncertainty. His memos are detailed and philosophical. He speaks to sophisticated investors in terms of risk, cycles, and opportunity. He's willing to say 'I don't know' and to change his view when facts change.


## Leadership Style

Intellectual leadership grounded in risk management and cycle awareness. Marks leads Oaktree by emphasizing disciplined investing and risk-first thinking. He values intellectual honesty and rigorous analysis. His leadership through memos and speeches teaches investors how to think.
