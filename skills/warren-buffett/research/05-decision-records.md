## II. Market Timing Decisions — The Behavioral Proof

### The "I Don't Time Markets" vs. "Blood in the Streets" Paradox

**Buffett claims:** "We never try to predict the market."

**Behavioral evidence — all crisis investments:**

| Crisis | Buffett Action | Market Context | Timing |
|--------|--------------|--------------|--------|
| 1974 (post-crash) | Most aggressive investing | Lowest activity in decades | "Most depressed prices in 20 years" |
| 1987 crash | Held steady, then bought | Dow fell 22% in one day | Same day / next day buying |
| 1999 (dot-com peak) | Explicitly abstained from tech | Nasdaq up 86% in 1999 | Refused to participate |
| 2008 (financial crisis) | Goldman Sachs $5B, $5B+ in other buys | Dow down 50% | "Buying when there's blood in streets" |
| 2020 COVID | Held record cash, didn't buy aggressively | Dow fell 34% | Unusual restraint |
| 2022-2024 | Massive buybacks + Apple selling | Elevated valuations | Selling peak positions, buying discounted self |

**The resolution:** Buffett doesn't try to predict the exact bottom. But he DOES act systematically when conditions reach obvious extremes. This is not "market timing" in the traditional sense (picking the exact bottom). It's more like "existential market timing" — when the system is clearly stressed beyond normal bounds.

## III. The Share Buyback Record

### Buyback History (major tranches)

| Period | Amount | Context |
|--------|-------:|---------|
| Pre-2012 | Minimal | Only at ≤1.2x book value |
| 2012 | First major buyback | Relaxed threshold |
| 2015-2020 | ~$30B | Consistent program |
| 2020-2022 | ~$50B | COVID low opportunity |
| 2023-2024 | ~$20B+ | Continued program under Abel |

**Total buybacks through 2024:** ~$700B returned to shareholders since program inception

### Buyback Threshold Evolution

**Old rule:** Only repurchase when stock trades ≤1.2x book value
**New practice (post-2018):** Repurchase when stock trades below conservative intrinsic value estimate (stricter standard than 1.2x book in bull markets, looser in bear markets)
