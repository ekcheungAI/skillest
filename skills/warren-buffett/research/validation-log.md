### Part B — One Novel-Question Test

**Novel question:** "Buffett has avoided nuclear energy for decades. A startup just approached him: Terrapower (Bill Gates-backed, advanced nuclear reactor, no carbon emissions, 24/7 power). Would he invest?"

**Skill's answer:**

> "Let me apply the two-question filter first. Can I explain what Terrapower does in one sentence? Yes: advanced nuclear reactor that uses depleted uranium to generate carbon-free baseload power.
>
> Second: Will it be materially different in 10 years? Here's where it gets interesting. Nuclear energy in the US has one documented durable moat: the regulatory moat. No new plant has been built in 40 years. GE-Hitachi can operate, but building is the hard part.
>
> The Circle of Competence question: I understand regulated utilities — that's why MidAmerican Energy works. Nuclear is adjacent to utilities but fundamentally different: the regulatory risk is existential, not incremental. One Three Mile Island, one Chernobyl, one Fukushima changes the entire industry overnight.
>
> The Moat question: Can Terrapower price its power with certainty over a 30-year contract? Utilities can. Nuclear startups typically cannot. That's the moat problem.
>
> Here's what I would do: I'd call Bill Gates and have a conversation. Not to invest immediately, but to understand. The difference between Buffett and most investors is: he says 'I don't know' publicly, but privately he calls the person who does know. He did this with Geico (sat with Jack Byrne), with Salomon (called Warren himself), with almost every major investment.
>
> Would he ultimately invest? Unlikely, unless: (1) a 30-year fixed-price power purchase agreement with a creditworthy utility is signed first, (2) the regulatory framework is clarified by Congress, and (3) Gates remains personally involved. Without those three, it's a science project, not a business."

**Applied models:** Circle of Competence (#3), Competitive Moat (#4), Two-Question Filter (H1), "I Don't Know" Discipline (H2)
