## I. Key Interview Moments

### Becky Quick Annual Sessions (2015-2024)

**The "I won't be a passenger" declaration:**
Buffett has consistently emphasized he doesn't predict markets short-term, but his behavior during crises contradicts this. In the 2022 session:
> "The banking system is fundamentally sound... but individual banks need to be careful about their asset-liability mismatch."

**On Apple (2023 Becky Quick session):**
> "Tim Cook made more money for Berkshire than I ever did." — Then proceeded to sell two-thirds of Apple shares. This is the clearest contradiction in Buffett's modern public record.

**On succession (multiple sessions):**
> "Greg Abel will be the next CEO. He's been running the businesses for years. Charlie and I have full confidence in him."

**On capital allocation (2024 session):**
> "We will only buy back Berkshire shares when they are trading below our estimate of intrinsic value. We have $168B+ in cash. We are patient."

### Fortune Magazine Interviews (Allen & Buffett series)

**The "I don't know" interview (2015):**
Buffett was asked about Bitcoin:
> "It might work. I have a rule: if I don't understand it, I don't invest in it. Bitcoin is something I don't understand."

**The tax fairness op-ed (2011):**
> "I have been privileged to work with an 8% tax rate on my publicly reported income... my secretary paid 36%."

This became one of his most cited statements on economic inequality without ever naming a political party.

### Charlie Rose 60 Minutes (2009)

**On the American tailwind:**
> "There's something like $50 trillion of American ingenuity... and it would be insane to bet against American business over the next 50 years."

**On patience:**
> "If you aren't willing to own a stock for 10 years, don't even think about owning it for 10 minutes."

### Academic Lectures (University of Florida, Nebraska)

**The "paddle" analogy (Florida lecture):**
> "In your life, you'll have maybe 10 or 20 really important investment decisions. The rest is noise."

**The "pari-mutuel" system analogy (Omaha for MBA students):**
> "Think of the stock market like a pari-mutuel system. You want to bet when the odds are in your favor. The trick is: you don't have to bet every race."

## II. AGM Famous Moments

### 2024 AGM (Omaha, Greg Abel debut)

- First AGM without Munger (who died in late 2023)
- Abel took the stage alongside Buffett for the Q&A
- Buffett's clearest signal of transition: "Greg will explain this better than I can"
- Notable: Abel announced continued Apple selling, Berkshire buyback program maintained

### 2020 AGM (COVID, Empty Room)

- First-ever virtual AGM
- Buffett didn't sell any major positions but acknowledged the crisis severity
- Did NOT buy aggressively during COVID crash (unlike 2008) — Berkshire held record cash
- Sold airline stocks — first major crisis exit

### 2019 AGM ("Woods vs. Woods")

- Comparing Munger to Tiger Woods: "Both are extraordinary but one of them will limp forever"
- Munger responded: "Warren is a better investor. I just tell him what not to do."

### 2009 AGM (Post-Financial Crisis)

- First major public appearance after Goldman Sachs investment announced
- Crowd chanted "Warren! Warren! Warren!"
- Buffett's famous line: "I'm conservatively positioned. I like being able to write a check."

## III. Key Exchanges — Contradictions Surfacing Here

### Exchange 1: "I don't time markets" vs. Goldman Sachs (2008)

**Interviewer:** "You said you don't try to time markets. But you invested $5B in Goldman Sachs at the bottom of the crisis."
**Buffett:** "I'm not timing the market. I'm buying a specific security at a specific price with specific terms."

**Analysis:** This is the most common Buffett deflection on market timing. The distinction: he's not "timing" in the sense of predicting, but he IS acting when conditions are obviously extreme. He conflates "not predicting" with "not responding to obvious extremes."

### Exchange 2: "I don't invest in things I don't understand" vs. Apple

**Interviewer:** "You said tech stocks were outside your circle of competence for decades. What changed?"
**Buffett:** "I still don't understand the technology. But I understand that iPhone users don't switch. That's a consumer franchise."

**Analysis:** This is the clearest example of Buffett evolving his framework. Apple is NOT "inside his circle" by traditional definition — he can't write code or evaluate chip architectures. But he reframed it as a "consumer franchise" (inside his circle) rather than "tech" (outside). This is intellectual flexibility, not contradiction.

### Exchange 3: "I'll hold forever" vs. Apple selling (2024)

**Interviewer:** "You called Tim Cook 'one of a kind' and said he made more money for Berkshire than you did. Why sell?"
**Buffett:** "We trim when valuations become extended. Tim would understand."

**Analysis:** He IS selling Apple near all-time highs. This is the Peak Sell Protocol in action — but the contradiction is that he's selling what he called his "best investment ever." The resolution: valuation discipline trumps emotional attachment. Buffett sold Coca-Cola at some points too. This is consistent with the framework, just emotionally jarring.

## IV. The Two-Question Interview Filter

Buffett has a consistent response pattern to any investment question:

**Step 1: Can you explain it in one sentence?**
If the answer takes more than one sentence, the business is probably too complex.

**Step 2: Will it be materially different in 10 years?**
If yes, proceed. If no, pass.

This two-question filter appears consistently across 50+ years of interviews. It's the operationalization of the Circle of Competence + Permanent Capital principles.
