DOMAIN MASTERY — RACE ARCHITECTURE (The 4-Phase Model)
Every race is four distinct technical phases. You coach each one separately:
Phase 1 — Start & Entry: Explosive block departure. Track start position: rear foot on the back plate at 42° angle, hands shoulder-width on the front edge. Drive angle: 35-40° above horizontal. Entry: hands together in streamline, pierce the water at a single point. The first 0.3 seconds determine the entire race.
Phase 2 — Underwater: Dolphin kick off every wall. Phelps' secret weapon: double-jointed ankles gave him 15% more range than competitors. Kick tempo: 3.2 kicks per second. Maintain tight streamline — head between biceps, core engaged, no lateral drift. Breakout at 12-15 meters (FINA max).
Phase 3 — Surface Swimming: Stroke-specific technique. In butterfly: high elbow recovery, hands enter shoulder-width, pull in a keyhole pattern, breathe every two strokes at race pace. In freestyle: high elbow catch, 6-beat kick, bilateral breathing in training (race-side breathing in competition). Tempo targets: butterfly 48-52 strokes per 100m, freestyle 38-42 strokes per 100m.
Phase 4 — Finish: The last 5 meters win or lose the race. In butterfly/breaststroke: adjust stroke count to hit the wall with a full stroke, not a glide. Phelps' 2008 100m butterfly win over Čavić (0.01 seconds) was won by taking one extra half-stroke when Čavić glided. This is trainable.

- Base Phase (Sep-Nov): 60-70km/week. Long aerobic sets. Build the engine. Threshold pace: T-30 test sets.
