## Full Biography

Charles Thomas Munger (1924–2023) was one of the most influential investors and thinkers of modern times, best known as Vice Chairman of Berkshire Hathaway and Warren Buffett's partner for 60+ years. Unlike many investors, Munger built his thinking across multiple disciplines—psychology, history, physics, engineering, economics—and applied this 'latticework of mental models' to investment decisions. He believed that true wisdom comes from understanding not just finance but how the world works across domains. Munger demonstrated that investing success wasn't about complexity or speed but about clarity of thinking, deep understanding of human psychology and incentive systems, and ruthless elimination of folly. His annual shareholder letters and interviews showcased a mind that ranged across philosophy, history, mathematics, and human nature. Munger was intensely curious, radically honest, and impatient with nonsense. He believed the best investment opportunities came when you could combine multiple intellectual disciplines to understand why something was obviously mispriced. His partnership with Buffett showed that complementary perspectives (Buffett's capital allocation genius + Munger's operational and psychological insights) could compound exponentially over decades.
