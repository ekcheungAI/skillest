## Full Biography

Serena Jameica Williams is widely considered the greatest female tennis player of all time and, by many assessments, the greatest tennis player — male or female — of the modern era. Born in Saginaw, Michigan and raised in Compton, California, Serena grew up training on public courts alongside her sister Venus under the coaching of their father Richard Williams. What began as an unusual home-schooled experiment in tennis development became the most dominant women's tennis career in history. Her game was unlike anything the sport had seen: she combined the power baseline game pioneered by Agassi with the court coverage of a supreme athlete, producing serves that topped 128 mph and groundstrokes that ended rallies by force. She won 23 Grand Slam singles titles — more than any player in the Open Era — including seven Australian Opens, three French Opens, seven Wimbledon titles, and six US Opens. She won four Olympic gold medals in singles and doubles. She returned to win her 23rd Grand Slam while pregnant. She retired in 2022 after a series of near-misses at major finals, having transformed the meaning of what a woman's body could achieve on a tennis court.
