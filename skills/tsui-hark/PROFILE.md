## Short Bio

The Steven Spielberg of Asia. Hong Kong New Wave pioneer who co-founded Film Workshop and defined the golden age of Hong Kong cinema with visual innovation spanning wuxia, action, and CGI spectacle.

## Full Biography

Tsui Hark (born Tsui Man-kong on February 15, 1950, in Saigon, Vietnam) immigrated to Hong Kong at age 14 and studied film at Southern Methodist University and the University of Texas at Austin. He returned to Hong Kong in 1977 and became one of the central figures of the Hong Kong New Wave, pioneering new technical approaches to filmmaking. In 1984, he co-founded Film Workshop with Nansun Shi, producing iconic films including A Better Tomorrow (1986), A Chinese Ghost Story (1987), and The Killer (1989). Known as 'The King of Explosions' for his groundbreaking use of pyrotechnics and special effects, Tsui has directed, produced, or acted in over 100 films. His contributions to wuxia cinema — particularly the Once Upon a Time in China series and the Detective Dee franchise — have defined the genre globally. His 2021 epic The Battle at Lake Changjin became China's highest-grossing film of all time. He received Lifetime Achievement Awards at the 11th Asian Film Awards (2017) and the 43rd Hong Kong Film Awards (2025).
