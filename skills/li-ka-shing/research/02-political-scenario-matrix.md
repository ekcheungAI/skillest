# 02 — Political Scenario Matrix

## The Origin Story

Li Ka-shing's obsession with political risk was forged in childhood. Born 1928 in Chaozhou, he lived through:
- The Japanese invasion of China (1937-1945)
- The family's flight to Hong Kong (1940)
- His father's death from tuberculosis (1943)
- The Communist revolution (1949)
- The Korean War embargo (which devastated Hong Kong's trade, 1950-1953)
- The 1967 Hong Kong riots
- The 1989 Tiananmen crackdown
- The 1997 Hong Kong handover
- The 2020 National Security Law
- The 2025-2026 Panama Ports diplomatic crisis

**The lesson repeated across seven decades:** political events can permanently destroy wealth in ways that no business analysis can predict. Therefore political risk must be treated as the **primary** risk, not a footnote.

## The Matrix

Before any major capital deployment, CK Hutchison's internal analysis produces a scenario matrix:

### Axis 1 — Political Scenarios

| Scenario | Probability weight | Example trigger |
|---|---|---|
| Current government continues | Base case | No election, no regime change |
| Opposition wins cleanly | Medium | Democratic transition, policy shift |
| Regime change / crisis | Low but non-zero | Coup, revolution, systemic collapse |
| Geopolitical rupture | Tail risk | Trade war, sanctions, capital controls, war |

### Axis 2 — Asset Outcomes Under Each Scenario

For every scenario, four outcomes are tracked:
1. **Book value preserved?** — Can the asset's accounting value survive the scenario?
2. **Operational cash flow intact?** — Does the business continue generating eggs?
3. **Exit liquidity available?** — Can we sell the asset and repatriate capital?
4. **Expropriation risk?** — Can a government seize the asset under any legal theory?

## The 30% Rule

**No single cell in the matrix can contain more than 30% of the portfolio's value.**

If Beijing, Washington, London, Ottawa, and Brussels can each only harm 30% of your assets, no single political event is existential. This is why CK Hutchison is a conglomerate spanning 50+ countries — it is not pursuing "synergies," it is pursuing political survivability.

The Li Ka Shing Foundation is not charity. It is the political goodwill layer that makes the matrix work.

**The "Third Son" concept:** Li has publicly described the Li Ka Shing Foundation as his "third son" — given the same care and resources as Victor Li and Richard Li. This framing signals that philanthropy is not discretionary — it is a core business function with its own dedicated governance.

Each donation builds a relationship with political, academic, and media elites in that jurisdiction. When a crisis hits (e.g., the 2025-2026 Panama ports dispute), Li has decades of accumulated goodwill to draw on in every major capital.

## The Live Test Case — Panama Ports 2025-2026

See `07-panama-ports-2025-2026.md` for the full timeline. Short version:

- **March 2025:** CK Hutchison announces $22.8B sale of 43 global ports to a BlackRock-led consortium (including MSC's TiL), including two Panama Canal terminals (Balboa and Cristobal).
- **July 2025:** Deal exclusivity period expires. Beijing signals displeasure — the Panama ports are viewed as strategically important to China's Belt and Road ambitions.
- **January 2026:** Hutchison reviews options to **split the deal** — potentially allowing Chinese state-owned COSCO to acquire larger stakes in selected terminals. Classic political scenario matrix adjustment: restructure the deal so that no single government's hostile action can kill it.
- **March 2026:** BlackRock-backed consortium pushes to close the deal **without** the two Panama terminals — accepting a reduced scope rather than walking away.

**What this case teaches:** The political scenario matrix is not static. When a scenario shifts from "base case" to "active hostile pressure," the response is to **restructure the transaction** — not to fight it, not to walk away, but to re-architect the deal so that it works under the new political scenario. This is Li Ka-shing's framework in live operation, executed by Victor Li under direct scrutiny from Washington and Beijing simultaneously.

## The Drill for Coaching Students

Map your own exposure to political scenarios:

1. List every asset and its primary jurisdiction.
2. For each asset, identify the 3 most realistic political scenarios.
3. Score each scenario × asset combination on the 4 outcome dimensions.
4. Sum the concentration in each scenario cell.
5. **If any single cell exceeds 30% of net worth, you are politically under-diversified.**
6. Begin the hedge before you need it. After you need it is too late.
