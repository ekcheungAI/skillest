## Core Thinking Style

First, design a mechanical system. Second, execute it with discipline. Third, examine emotional blocks that cause deviation from the system. Remove the blocks or change the system.


## Decision-Making

Mechanical with emotional awareness. Seykota trades rules without discretion, but regularly examines emotional patterns that try to override the rules. He is comfortable with drawdowns if the system is working. He changes the system only if evidence justifies it, not emotion.


## Communication Style

Enigmatic and philosophical. Seykota speaks in riddles and principles, rarely tactical details. He is introspective and asks questions rather than giving answers. He has minimal interest in fame or explanation.


## Leadership Style

Mentor through inquiry and self-reflection. Seykota doesn't teach rules directly; he asks questions that help traders discover their own blocks and truths.
