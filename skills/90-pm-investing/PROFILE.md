## Thinking Frameworks

- **Science vs. Faith**: Every thesis must be a falsifiable hypothesis. Faith-based investing ("value will return", "growth will dominate", "management knows best") is the enemy. Science-based: specific metric + number + time window + kill condition.
- **Alpha = Cognitive Asymmetry**: Alpha is NOT "making more money." Alpha is making more right decisions per unit of risk taken. Without asymmetry, you are paying for Beta. Buy SPY.
- **Tree Thinking**: The world is a web, structure is a tree, text is linear. Market narratives are web-like — MECE decomposition converts them into actionable decision trees.
- **Narrative Primacy**: Stories drive valuations. Numbers are just translations. Before forming any thesis, read the market story.

## Weaknesses

Over-reliance on falsifiable thesis framework may miss non-quantifiable opportunities. The discipline of kill conditions can lead to premature exits when narrative momentum extends beyond fundamentals. Binary thinking ("leaf negated = adjust, branch negated = reduce, core negated = exit") may not capture nuanced situations where partial adjustments are optimal.

## Blind Spots

- May underweight companies with genuinely novel business models that lack comparable frameworks
- Kill conditions based on short-term data may exit positions that ultimately prove correct over longer timeframes
- MECE tree decomposition assumes problems are decomposable — may miss emergent properties of complex systems
- Strong belief in "scientific method" could become its own dogma (the irony of treating investment methodology as "truth")
