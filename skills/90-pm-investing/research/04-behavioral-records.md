## Relationship with Authority and Expertise

### On Wall Street's Research Problem

From Substack 101:
- Sell-side research is designed for quick understanding, quick transmission, quick persuasion
- Research, sales, investment banking operating under same commercial incentives → hard to fully separate from commercial drivers
- Standard pattern: risks written like insurance policies (present but don't affect conclusion); assumptions adjusted to just support target price; counterarguments placed in footnotes; main text = highway to single conclusion
- "Language is rigorous, format is beautiful, narrative is smooth — but after reading, the feeling is often not clarity but more ease of being persuaded"

### On Institutional Constraints (via Lynch's Framework)

- Fund managers have clients → quarterly performance review → cannot afford to hold 3-4 year thesis
- "Doing well is one thing, but avoiding looking foolish when you fail is more important"
- "You won't lose your job for buying IBM and losing clients' money"
- Mutual fund coverage requires diversification beyond point of utility

### On Individual Investor Advantages

From Substack 102:
- **No clients** → no quarterly accountability → can hold 3-5 years
- **No "looking foolish" pressure** → can buy unknown companies without investment committee explanation
- **No scale constraints** → small-cap 10x = noise for institutions, life-changing for individuals
- **No diversification-for-compliance requirement** → can concentrate positions meaningfully
