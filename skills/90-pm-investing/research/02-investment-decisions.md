# 90-pm-investing — Investment Decisions & Case Studies

## The Two Defining Trades

### Trade 1: OXY (2020-2022) — The Education

**Thesis:**
> "Oil demand is temporarily depressed, not permanently destroyed."

**Entry:**
- Purchased OXY (Occidental Petroleum) common stock and long LEAPS
- Entry price: ~$10-20 per share
- Context: WTI crude oil fell to -$37.63 on April 20, 2020 (first negative oil price in history)
- Market narrative: "Oil is dead" — ESG outflows + pandemic demand collapse

**The Tree:**

```
Core Question: If oil demand returns to normal within 12 months, should OXY go from $10 to $40-70?

├── Supply Side
│   ├── Existing Supply
│   │   ├── Domestic (US): US production fell from 12.8M bbl/day to 10.0M bbl/day (-22%) — below breakeven
│   │   └── International (OPEC+): Would OPEC+ cuts reach 80%+ execution rate?
│   └── New Supply
│       ├── Domestic (US): CapEx cut in half; rig count collapsed
│       └── International (OPEC+): New project FIDs all postponed/cancelled
│
└── Demand Side
    ├── Short-term: Would pandemic lockdowns lift and transport/industrial demand return?
    └── Long-term: Would EVs accelerate oil demand peak before 2030-2035?
```

**Kill Condition (Set Before Entry):**
> "If global oil demand is still below 90% of 2019 levels within 12 months post-pandemic → EXIT."

**Key Data Points Tracked:**
- EIA monthly production reports → US output actual: -22% (exceeded -15% assumption → 🟢)
- Baker Hughes weekly rig count → fell >50% (exceeded assumption → 🟢)
- OPEC monthly reports → compliance rates
- IEA monthly oil market reports → demand recovery trajectory
- China refinery run rates, IATA flight data

**Result:**
- WTI crude recovered from -$37.63 to ~$40 by July 2020
- OXY reached $70+ by 2022
- Sold all LEAPS at profit; retained common stock as a souvenir
- First bucket: confirmed

**Lessons Extracted:**
- Falsifiable thesis + disciplined kill condition = repeatable process
- Short-term demand recovery thesis was correct; long-term structural concerns (EVs) were noted but separated
- The trade was ~99.99% confidence given the asymmetry of the setup

### Trade 2: GME (2021) — The Tuition

**Thesis:**
> "This is the next Volkswagen squeeze."

**Entry:**
- Bought heavily on GME short squeeze narrative
- Context: GME short interest reached 140% of float; Reddit WallStreetBets community; VW squeeze precedent (2008, Porsche squeezed 94% of float → 10x price increase)

**The Flawed Logic Chain (4 Links, All Must Hold):**

1. **Shorts must be forced to cover** — short interest 140% means they must eventually buy back shares
2. **Retail won't sell** — Reddit "Diamond Hands" consensus, supply locked up
3. **Brokers won't restrict trading** — retail can continue buying to maintain buy pressure
4. **Regulators won't intervene** — SEC and exchanges won't halt or modify trading rules

**Kill Condition: NONE SET.**

**What Actually Happened:**
- January 28, 2021: Clearinghouses raised margin requirements 250%
- Robinhood and Interactive Brokers simultaneously restricted buying
- Retail investors could only sell, not buy
- Squeeze mechanism ("buy pressure > supply") was physically destroyed
- GME crashed from ~$480 to ~$40

**Loss: HK$2M**

**Lessons Extracted:**
- "No thesis without a kill condition." The mechanism of the thesis must have a defined failure point.
- Four independent assumptions, all required to hold simultaneously → fragile chain
- Melvin Capital made the same mistake — their tree was incomplete (missing "short squeeze" branch entirely)
- The trade was a *slogan*, not a *hypothesis*. It couldn't be falsified because it had no specific data points to track.

## The Contrast: GME vs OXY

| Dimension | GME (Loss) | OXY (Win) |
|----------|------------|-----------|
| Thesis | "VW squeeze" (mechanism) | "Oil demand recovery" (fundamental) |
| Falsifiability | Zero | High |
| Kill Condition | None set | Specific: demand <90% of 2019 in 12 months |
| Assumption Count | 4 independent (fragile chain) | 1 core premise (robust) |
| Mechanism | Dependent on market structure | Dependent on fundamental recovery |
| Exit Discipline | None | Kill condition → exit |
| Result | -HK$2M | First bucket (life-changing) |

**Core Lesson:** "A thesis without a kill condition is a slogan. A slogan can't be falsified. If it can't be falsified, you can't know when to leave. If you can't leave, you're not investing — you're praying."

## EOSE Analysis (Short Case)

**Thesis:** "EOSE zinc battery technology cannot achieve scale by 2026-2028; current 40x PS valuation is unsupported."

**The Four-Branch Tree:**

```
├── Branch A: Demand — $22.6B pipeline conversion
│   └── Actual conversion rate ~0.28%. Fuzzy Panda FOIA: 80% of backlog = LOIs/contemplated contracts
│
├── Branch B: Technology feasibility — Z3 battery specs
│   └── RTE 75% vs LFP 90%; cost $250/kWh vs LFP $70/kWh (3.6x gap). HBr safety issues (FOIA)
│
├── Branch C: Scale-up — cash runway
│   └── Cash $58.7M = 2.6 months runway. DOE loan $303.5M, only $90.9M drawn
│
└── Branch D: Shareholder return — dilution and integrity
    └── 29x dilution since 2020. Cerberus 78% ownership; can short and hedge simultaneously.
        → 2026-03: Securities fraud class action filed (Nov 2025–Feb 2026 misleading statements)
        → Stock fell -39% post-lawsuit
```

**Result:** All four branches showed critical issues → conclusion: thesis (stock overvalued) confirmed by fundamentals. Short thesis validated by subsequent class action lawsuit.

## Decision Framework Summary

| Step | Action | Example (OXY) |
|------|--------|---------------|
| 1. Intuition | What does your industry knowledge tell you? | "Oil crashed but the world still uses oil" |
| 2. Hypothesis | Translate to: metric + number + time window + kill condition | "Oil demand returns to 90% of 2019 within 12 months → EXIT if not" |
| 3. MECE Tree | Decompose into mutually exclusive, collectively exhaustive branches | Supply (Existing/New × Domestic/Intl) + Demand (Short/Long) |
| 4. Leaf Nodes | One falsifiable hypothesis per leaf with 4 parts: H/D/C/K | H: US production -15%; D: EIA monthly; C: 🟢/-15%; K: production up 2 months at <$30 → 🔴 |
| 5. Feynman | Try to prove yourself wrong before market does | "EV adoption → permanent oil demand destruction?" → Tested: oil demand peak 2030-2035; thesis unaffected |
| 6. Track | Every quarter: data → conclusion → adjust or exit | Monthly EIA, OPEC reports, IEA demand data |
| 7. Exit | If kill condition triggered → EXIT. No exceptions. No negotiation. | "Demand <90% of 2019 within 12 months" → kill condition: DO NOT MODIFY after entry |

## Key Principles

1. **Kill conditions cannot be changed after entry.** Pre-commit to the exit condition before placing the trade.
2. **Fewer assumptions = stronger thesis.** OYX: 1 assumption. GME: 4 assumptions. The assumption chain is only as strong as its weakest link.
3. **Branch vs leaf corrections.** Leaf negated → adjust number, hold. Branch negated → reduce position. Core premise negated → EXIT immediately.
4. **Separation of timeframes.** OXY's thesis was short-term demand recovery. The long-term EV threat was acknowledged but separated — it didn't invalidate the near-term thesis.
5. **Document everything.** Pre-written kill conditions would have prevented the GME loss.
