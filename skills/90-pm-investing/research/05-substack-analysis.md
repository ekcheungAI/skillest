### 101: 前言 (Introduction) — The Manifesto

**Word count:** ~3,000
**Trust level:** Primary-authored (free tier)
**Core argument:** The investment world is broken. Most financial content is designed to sell, not to inform. Individual investors are structurally disadvantaged by time, but the solution isn't more information — it's a framework.

**Key arguments:**

1. **The "next Nvidia" trap:** Reader sees KOL recommendation → reads YouTube videos → follows forum posts → buys → stock halves → can't explain what went wrong. Problem: not effort, but direction of effort.

2. **The problem isn't information:** Individual investors in 2026 have access to the same financial statements, earnings calls, and sell-side reports as institutions. Information is not the bottleneck.

3. **The bottleneck is framework:** "What you lack isn't more information. It's a system to filter information, deconstruct stories, and verify hypotheses."

4. **Tree thinking vs story thinking:**
   - The world = web (everything connected)
   - Structure = tree (decomposed, actionable)
   - Text = linear (how we communicate)

5. **"Science over faith":** Faith-based = "value will return", "growth will dominate", "management knows best." Science-based = falsifiable hypothesis with kill conditions.

6. **The WoW guild origin:** Knowledge sharing builds collective intelligence. "When the whole guild gets stronger, we can take down the hardest bosses together." → Community-driven alpha.

7. **Free by design:** "This is never a business for me." The guild model means free knowledge sharing. The product tiers (skill, tracking system) are for committed users who want the full system.

**Vocabulary introduced:** 相互獨立，完全窮盡 (MECE), 科學大於信仰 (Science over faith), 門留一條縫 (Leave the door slightly open)

### 102: 北極星 (North Star) — Why Do Research?

**Word count:** ~4,000
**Trust level:** Primary-authored (free tier)
**Core argument:** If the goal is "more money," just buy SPY (8-10% annual, no research needed). Research is only justified if the goal is Alpha — returns above Beta, per unit of risk taken.

**Key arguments:**

1. **Beta = market salary:** Every unit of risk taken earns exactly Beta. Sharpe's CAPM: E(Ri) = Rf + β × (market risk premium). Beta pays a salary. You cannot earn more than Beta without taking more risk — unless you earn Alpha.

2. **79% of fund managers underperform:** SPIVA 2025 data. This isn't news — it's structural. Most "professionals" can't beat an index over 15 years.

3. **Alpha = cognitive asymmetry:** You know something the market doesn't, AND you can prove the consensus is wrong at a specific node. Without asymmetry: you are paying for Beta. Buy SPY.

4. **Three reasons traditional approaches fail:**
   - **Value investing:** Correct principle (buy below intrinsic value), broken method (菸蒂 disappeared, P/B no longer correlates with cheapness). Graham used statistical screens; when the screens stopped working, the method degraded.
   - **Technical analysis:** Not falsifiable. Popper's criterion: a theory must be testable. TA can never be proven wrong (failed signal → "use different indicator"). Same logic as astrology.
   - **Quant:** Infrastructure arms race requires $300K-$1M minimum. Capacity constraints make Renaissance's Medallion unreplicable. Even Renaissance couldn't replicate Medallion in their external funds.

5. **Individual investor structural advantages:**
   - No clients → no quarterly accountability → can hold 3-5 years
   - No "looking foolish" pressure → can buy unknown companies
   - No scale constraints → small-cap 10x = life-changing, not noise
   - No diversification-for-compliance requirements → can concentrate

6. **But freedom ≠ ability:** Your brain is a "story generator." It seeks narratives that feel good, not narratives that are true. Lynch's "buy what you know" means: use industry knowledge to generate hypotheses, not convictions.

7. **Peter Lynch's discipline:** Industry knowledge → hypothesis generation. Framework → falsification. NOT: industry knowledge → confirmation bias.

**Key insight:** "Your industry knowledge gives you the ability to generate hypotheses — you see things analysts don't. But a hypothesis isn't truth. A hypothesis is a proposition that must be verified or falsified through structured methods."

### 103: 故事 (Story) — The Five-Signal Framework

**Word count:** ~4,500
**Trust level:** Primary-authored (may be behind paywall)
**Core argument:** Before forming a thesis, detect the market's current narrative. The Five-Signal Framework reveals what the market believes, so you can identify where consensus might be wrong.

**Key arguments:**

1. **Hans Rosling fact quiz:** 13 questions about the world. Average human scores below random chance (33%). This isn't ignorance — it's systematic cognitive bias. The same biases drive markets.

2. **Collective wisdom vs collective inertia:** Markets are usually efficient — thousands of investors seeking errors. But independent thinking has a cost (being wrong alone). When incentives favor consensus over correctness, markets degrade from "wisdom" to "inertia." Both look like "market consensus" from outside.

3. **Stories drive valuations:** Every valuation is a story first. The story determines framework, peer group, multiple, and assumptions. Numbers translate the story into Excel. If the story is wrong, precision in calculation = precisely wrong.

4. **Five-Signal Framework:**
   - Signal 1: Valuation metric selection (P/E vs EV/Revenue vs P/B reveals analyst's story about company identity)
   - Signal 2: Peer group mapping (Ferrari vs Toyota vs Hermès = 3x P/E multiple difference)
   - Signal 3: Earnings call Q&A themes (question direction shift = narrative evolution)
   - Signal 4: Fund flows and positioning (ETF classification, growth vs value drift)
   - Signal 5: One-line media tag (compressed narrative, watch for "forever" language)

5. **Six narrative errors:**
   - **Identity Error:** NVDA treated as semiconductor (INTC/AMD peers) vs AI infrastructure platform (MSFT/GOOGL peers)
   - **Permanence Error:** Oil is dead (2020) vs growth stocks' pandemic acceleration was permanent (2021)
   - **Lifecycle Mismatch:** Tech company priced at wrong lifecycle stage
   - **Narrative Inertia:** FB mobile monetization — data changed, story didn't
   - **Discrete Event Pricing:** Binary probability pricing instead of expected value
   - **Macro Contagion:** Sector narrative suppressing individuals regardless of fundamentals

6. **"Consistent ≠ correct":** In 2006, CDO market signals all pointed one direction — "US housing prices won't fall nationally." Every signal consistent. The story was wrong. Michael Burry read the underlying mortgages one by one and found the error.

**Key insight:** "You don't need to be smarter than the market. You only need to be more correct than the market consensus at one specific node."

### 104: 假設 (Hypothesis) — The Falsification Method

**Word count:** ~5,000
**Trust level:** Primary-authored (free tier)
**Core argument:** Every thesis must be a falsifiable hypothesis. Feynman invented the method. GME lost HK$2M because it had no kill condition. OXY made a life-changing return because it did.

**Key arguments:**

1. **The three types of believers:** Value (faith that "value will return"), Growth (faith that "TAM = certainty"), Management (faith that "CEO knows best"). All three diseases: never ask "what situation would make me admit I'm wrong."

2. **The GME loss (HK$2M):** Thesis = "short squeeze like VW." Logic chain required 4 simultaneous assumptions: shorts forced to cover + retail won't sell + brokers won't restrict + regulators won't intervene. No kill condition set. January 28, 2021: Robinhood blocked buying → mechanism destroyed → thesis dead → still held.

3. **The Charles de Vaulx tragedy:** 59-year-old, $20B AUM fund manager. Value investing believer. Fund declined, 2021 liquidation, April 2026: jumped from office. "He felt he had failed to defend the mission of value investing." Identity merged with methodology. When methodology failed, identity failed with it.

4. **Feynman's four-step method:**
   1. Guess → hypothesis
   2. Compute → predictions
   3. Compare → data vs predictions
   4. Conclude → if doesn't match → WRONG

5. **"The door must be kept slightly open":** Feynman: "You must allow yourself to possibly not have it all right. Otherwise, you may never solve it."

6. **The Feynman challenge:** 1986 Rogers Commission. NASA said shuttle failure probability = 1 in 100,000. Feynman used ice water and a rubber O-ring to show real probability ≈ 1 in 100. NASA had the data — they didn't want to see it.

7. **Four truths of physics (from 104):**
   - Aristotle (2000 years of "truth") → wrong
   - Galileo (experiment) → improved
   - Newton (230 years of "truth") → approximation
   - Einstein → improved further
   - Quantum mechanics → still evolving

   **Lesson:** Truth doesn't exist. Only "hypotheses that haven't been falsified yet." Investment frameworks = hypotheses, not truths.

8. **Why the hypothesis must be the opposite of consensus:** If your view matches consensus, you only earn Beta. Buy SPY. True Alpha requires betting on a market-unpriced future. OXY: consensus said "oil is dead" → thesis said "temporary demand depression" → 7x return when narrative reset.

9. **The four-step thesis construction:**
   1. Intuition → translate to specific metric + number + time window + kill condition
   2. Minimize premises (OXY: 1 premise. GME: 4 premises = fragile chain)
   3. Try to falsify (become the short seller of your own thesis)
   4. Track continuously (假設有保質期 — hypotheses have expiration dates)

**Key quote:** "科學家主動找證據推翻自己，信徒主動找證據支持自己" (Scientists actively seek evidence to disprove themselves; believers actively seek evidence to support themselves.)

### 105: 畫樹 (Drawing Trees) — MECE + The Three Methods

**Word count:** ~6,000
**Trust level:** Primary-authored (behind paywall)
**Core argument:** Once you have a falsifiable hypothesis, decompose it into a MECE decision tree. Each leaf node is a testable claim. Every quarter, run the experiment. Adjust or exit.

**Key arguments:**

1. **Why not a checklist?**
   - Checklist = fixed. Every investment thesis is different → tree adapts.
   - Checklist = flat. Some factors are fatal, some are additive → tree has hierarchy.
   - Checklist = no conclusion. 9 green, 4 red → buy or not? → tree has direction.
   - Checklist = can't be killed. 4 red but "other 9 still okay" → tree has brakes.

2. **MECE grammar:**
   - **Mutually Exclusive:** Branches don't overlap. (Analysis: "CUDA strong" + "developer loyalty" + "software moat" = 1 reason, 3 descriptions. Not ME.)
   - **Collectively Exhaustive:** Branches cover all possibilities. Melvin Capital's GME tree missed "short squeeze" entirely — that missing branch killed them.

3. **Three drawing methods:**

   **Method 1 — Formula:** Revenue = A × B × C. Naturally exhaustive (it's an identity). Best for: companies with clear financial formulas (Meta = DAP × Time × Ad Load × Yield).

   **Method 2 — Process:** Sequential milestones. Best for: industrial, infrastructure, companies with clear stages (EOSE: Demand → Technology → Scale-up → Shareholder Return).

   **Method 3 — X vs Non-X:** Supply vs Demand. Mathematically MECE by definition. Best for: commodities, supply/demand markets (OXY: Supply (Existing/New × Domestic/Intl) + Demand (Short/Long term)).

4. **The four parts of a leaf node:**
   - **Hypothesis:** "[Metric] [direction] [number] within [time window]"
   - **Data:** "Check [specific report]"
   - **Conclusion:** 🟢 confirmed | 🟡 direction right, magnitude uncertain | 🔴 negated
   - **Kill Condition:** "If [metric] [number] for [time window] → EXIT"

5. **Example: OXY tree (complete):**
```
Core Question: If oil demand returns to 2019 levels within 12 months, should OXY go $10 → $40-70?
├── Supply
│   ├── Existing × Domestic → US production -22% → 🟢 → Kill: production up 2 months at <$30
│   ├── Existing × International → OPEC+ compliance? → 🟡 → Kill: compliance <60% for 2 months
│   ├── New × Domestic → Rig count -50%+ → 🟢 → Kill: rig count up at <$30 oil
│   └── New × International → Project FIDs postponed → 🟢 → Kill: FIDs restart at <$40
└── Demand
    ├── Short-term → Demand to 90% of 2019 in 12 months → 🟢 → Kill: still <85% at 12 months → EXIT
    └── Long-term → EV → Not falsified (not needed for short-term thesis)
```

6. **Three correction postures:**
   - Leaf negated → adjust number, hold
   - Branch negated → re-evaluate position size
   - Core premise negated → EXIT immediately

7. **The Science/Art divide:**
   - **Science:** Tree drawing, MECE decomposition, falsifiability, kill conditions
   - **Art:** Position sizing, risk tolerance, time horizon, conviction level

8. **"Everyone is ultimately their own 90s PM":** The framework is free. The judgment is personal. The tree won't sign the order — you will.

**Key quote:** "Howard Marks said: 'We may never know where we're going, but we'd better have a good idea where we are.' The tree tells you where you are. It doesn't tell you how much to bet."

## Cross-Article Framework Summary

### The Complete 90PM Investment Process

```
1. DETECT MARKET STORY (103: Five-Signal Framework)
   ├── Check valuation metric consensus
   ├── Map peer group and drift
   ├── Read earnings Q&A themes
   ├── Track fund flows
   └── Decode one-line media tag
   
2. IDENTIFY NARRATIVE ERROR (103: Six Error Types)
   └── Which of: Identity / Permanence / Lifecycle / Inertia / Discrete / Macro?
   
3. FORM HYPOTHESIS (104: Feynman Falsification)
   ├── Translate intuition → metric + number + time window + kill condition
   ├── Minimize premises (fewer = stronger)
   ├── Try to prove wrong (Feynman test)
   └── Write kill condition BEFORE entry
   
4. DRAW DECISION TREE (105: MECE Methods)
   ├── Choose method: Formula / Process / X vs Non-X
   ├── Expand with brainstorming (X vs Non-X dimensions)
   ├── Build leaf nodes (H/D/C/K × 4 parts)
   └── Connect leaves to core thesis
   
5. TRACK AND CORRECT (105: Correction Postures)
   ├── Quarterly: data → conclusion → adjustment
   ├── Leaf negated → adjust number, hold
   ├── Branch negated → reduce position
   └── Core premise negated → EXIT immediately
```
