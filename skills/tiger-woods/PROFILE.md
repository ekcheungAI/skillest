## Full Biography

Eldrick Tont 'Tiger' Woods is widely considered one of the greatest golfers of all time and the athlete who transformed golf's global profile. Born in Cypress, California in 1975 to a Thai mother and African-American father, Woods was a prodigy — featured on The Mike Douglas Show at age 2, won his first US Amateur at 18, and turned pro at 20. Over his career, he won 15 major championships (second only to Jack Nicklaus's 18), 82 PGA Tour events (tied for most with Sam Snead), and spent 683 weeks at world No. 1 (a record). His dominance was physical and psychological — he transformed his body to generate unprecedented power, then transformed golf's mental dimension with his cold focus. His personal life imploded in 2009-2010 in a scandal that cost him endorsements and public image, and his body has been ravaged by injuries — multiple knee surgeries, spinal fusions, and a car accident in 2021 that nearly killed him. And yet he returned to win the 2019 Masters — arguably the greatest sporting comeback in history. His legacy is as a transcendent talent who broke every convention of his sport.
