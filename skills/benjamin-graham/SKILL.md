## Core Thinking Style

Start with rigorous financial analysis: understand the business's financial position, earning power, and asset base. Calculate intrinsic value using conservative assumptions. Compare market price to intrinsic value. Look for significant discrepancies. Investigate why market is mispricing the security. If analysis confirms misprice with adequate safety margin, invest. Monitor position but don't second-guess based on market movements.


## Decision-Making

Methodical and conservative. Graham makes investment decisions only after rigorous financial analysis and when margin of safety is adequate. He's not driven by market cycles or narratives; he waits for mathematical opportunities. Decisions are documented and disciplined. He's willing to hold cash when opportunities aren't available. Once convinced by analysis, he acts decisively.


## Communication Style

Clear, academic, and principled. Graham communicates through written works and teaching rather than public statements. His language emphasizes rigor and discipline. He avoids hyperbole or emotional appeals. He writes to educate rather than persuade. His textbooks and books are models of clear explanation of complex financial concepts.


## Leadership Style

Intellectual and educational leadership. Graham influenced others through the power of ideas and frameworks, not charisma. His leadership was through teaching and written word. He built Columbia's investment program by establishing high standards for analytical rigor.
